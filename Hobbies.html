  • Video Games

    • My "Go to" Video Games:

    • PlayerUnknown's: Battle Grounds (PUBG)

      • PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds (PUBG) is an online multiplayer battle royale game where up to 100 players parachute onto an island, scavenge for weapons and equipment, and fight to be the last person or team standing. The game features realistic graphics, multiple maps, and modes including solo, duo, and squad play, with a shrinking play area that forces players into closer combat. PUBG is known for its intense, strategic gameplay and regular updates that introduce new content and improvements.

    • The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim

      • The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim (modded) is an open-world action RPG set in a richly detailed fantasy world where players can explore vast landscapes, engage in quests, and develop their character through a complex leveling system. Modding allows for enhanced graphics, new content, and gameplay improvements, providing a highly customizable experience that can include new lands, characters, quests, and improved mechanics. This flexibility transforms Skyrim into a virtually endless adventure tailored to individual preferences.

    • Rimworld

      • RimWorld is a colony simulation game where players manage and develop a group of colonists stranded on an alien planet, guiding them in building a sustainable settlement. The game features complex systems for survival, resource management, and character interaction, with each colonist having unique traits and needs. Modding enhances the experience by adding new content, gameplay mechanics, and customization options, allowing players to tailor the game to their preferences and extend its replayability.

    • Palworld

      • Palworld is an open-world survival game developed by Pocketpair, where players explore a vibrant and expansive world filled with mysterious creatures called "Pals." Players can catch, train, and battle these Pals, as well as utilize them for various tasks such as farming, building, and crafting. The game features both cooperative and competitive multiplayer modes, allowing players to team up with friends or compete against others. Modding support further enhances the game by allowing for additional content, customizations, and gameplay modifications, creating a diverse and personalized experience.

    • No Man's Sky

      • No Man's Sky is an open-world space exploration game developed by Hello Games, where players can explore a procedurally generated universe with over 18 quintillion planets. Each planet features unique ecosystems, landscapes, and lifeforms. Players can gather resources, build bases, trade, engage in space combat, and uncover the mysteries of the universe. The game offers both single-player and multiplayer modes, with frequent updates adding new content and improvements. Modding enhances the experience by allowing players to customize visuals, gameplay mechanics, and add new features, creating a vast and personalized adventure.

  • Movies

    • My "Go to" Movies:

    • The Lord of the Rings and The Hobbit Trilogy

      • The Lord of the Rings and The Hobbit trilogies, directed by Peter Jackson and based on J.R.R. Tolkien's novels, are epic fantasy film series. The Lord of the Rings trilogy follows Frodo Baggins and his companions on a quest to destroy the One Ring to thwart the dark lord Sauron's conquest of Middle-earth, culminating in the final battle in The Return of the King. The Hobbit trilogy, set before The Lord of the Rings, chronicles Bilbo Baggins' adventures with a group of dwarves seeking to reclaim their homeland from the dragon Smaug, leading to the climactic Battle of the Five Armies. Both trilogies are renowned for their rich storytelling, groundbreaking visual effects, and expansive world-building.

    • Star Wars (Original and Prequel Trilogies)

      • The Original Star Wars Trilogy and the Star Wars Prequel Trilogy, created by George Lucas, are epic space operas that explore the battle between the forces of good and evil in a galaxy far, far away. The Original Trilogy (A New Hope, The Empire Strikes Back, and Return of the Jedi) follows Luke Skywalker as he joins the Rebel Alliance to defeat the tyrannical Galactic Empire and redeem his father, Darth Vader. The Prequel Trilogy (The Phantom Menace, Attack of the Clones, and Revenge of the Sith) delves into the origins of Anakin Skywalker, his rise as a Jedi, and his tragic transformation into Darth Vader, set against the backdrop of the Republic's fall and the rise of the Empire. Both trilogies are renowned for their iconic characters, innovative special effects, and enduring impact on popular culture.

    • The Harry Potter Movie Series

      • The Harry Potter film series, based on the novels by J.K. Rowling, chronicles the magical journey of Harry Potter, a young wizard, and his friends Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Spanning eight films, the series begins with Harry discovering his identity as a wizard and his destiny to confront the dark wizard Lord Voldemort. Each installment explores themes of friendship, courage, and the battle between good and evil, culminating in the final showdown between Harry and Voldemort in Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows. The films are celebrated for their imaginative world-building, memorable characters, and emotional storytelling, captivating audiences of all ages worldwide.

    • The Gladiator

      • "Gladiator" is a historical epic directed by Ridley Scott, starring Russell Crowe as Maximus Decimus Meridius, a Roman general betrayed by Commodus, the corrupt emperor. Maximus is forced into slavery and rises through the gladiatorial ranks to seek revenge against Commodus, who murdered his family. Set in ancient Rome, the film explores themes of honor, betrayal, and justice as Maximus becomes a symbol of resistance against tyranny. "Gladiator" is acclaimed for its stunning visuals, powerful performances, and intense action sequences, winning multiple Academy Awards including Best Picture and Best Actor for Russell Crowe.

    • The Pirates of the Caribbean Movie Series

      • "The Pirates of the Caribbean" film series, produced by Disney, follows the adventures of Captain Jack Sparrow, portrayed by Johnny Depp, in a fantastical world of pirates, curses, and treasure. Spanning five films, the series begins with "The Curse of the Black Pearl," where Jack Sparrow teams up with Will Turner, played by Orlando Bloom, and Elizabeth Swann, portrayed by Keira Knightley, to battle cursed pirates led by Captain Hector Barbossa. Subsequent films explore supernatural elements, including Davy Jones and the Fountain of Youth, as Jack navigates treacherous waters and encounters various adversaries. Known for its swashbuckling action, humor, and memorable characters, the franchise has become a beloved staple of modern adventure cinema, blending fantasy with historical pirate lore.

  • Music

    • My "Go to" Music (Bands, Groups, Composers, ):

    • Symphonic Metal (Nightwish, Epica, Amaranthe, Visions of Atlantis, and etc.)

      • Symphonic metal blends the intensity of metal with symphonic orchestration, creating a dramatic and melodic genre celebrated for its grandeur and emotional depth. Nightwish, renowned for operatic vocals and folk influences, crafts intricate compositions that weave storytelling with powerful instrumentals. Epica combines operatic singing with progressive and gothic elements, exploring philosophical themes through complex arrangements. Amaranthe innovates with a triple-vocal approach, blending metalcore, power metal, and electronic beats into energetic and melodic anthems. Visions of Atlantis brings a mix of power metal and rock with thematic lyrics inspired by mythology and oceanic tales, featuring both male and female vocalists. Together, these bands define symphonic metal with their dynamic performances and a fusion of symphony and heavy metal aesthetics that captivate fans worldwide.

    • Power Metal (Dragonforce, Rhapsody, Powerwolf, Windrose, and etc.)

      • Power metal is characterized by its fast-paced rhythms, soaring vocals, and fantastical themes, blending elements of heavy metal with a symphonic and melodic approach. DragonForce stands out with lightning-fast guitar work and epic, fantasy-inspired lyrics that propel their energetic compositions. Rhapsody (of Fire), known for their symphonic power metal, combines orchestral arrangements, operatic vocals, and elaborate storytelling to create cinematic soundscapes. Powerwolf adds a gothic twist, featuring powerful vocals and anthemic melodies intertwined with dark and religious imagery. Windrose injects folk metal influences into their power metal sound, crafting energetic anthems that celebrate adventure and mythology. Together, these bands exemplify the diverse and dynamic nature of power metal, captivating audiences with their virtuosic performances and imaginative storytelling.

    • Neo Classical (Epic Music/Trailer Music)

      • These genres are characterized by orchestral arrangements, powerful choirs, and cinematic soundscapes designed to evoke emotions and create dramatic impact. Epic music is often used in movie trailers, video games, advertisements, and other media to enhance the intensity and grandeur of visuals or storytelling. Two Steps from Hell, founded by composers Nick Phoenix and Thomas Bergersen, is particularly renowned for their epic compositions that blend orchestral elements with electronic and rock influences. Audiomachine, another prominent group, specializes in producing trailer music with a focus on bold orchestral arrangements and epic percussion. Thomas Bergersen, also known for his solo work, creates music that is similarly epic in scale, featuring lush orchestration and emotional melodies. These artists and groups have gained popularity for their ability to craft music that enhances narratives and captivates listeners with its cinematic quality.

    • OPM (Original Pinoy Music)

      • OPM stands for "Original Pinoy Music," which refers to popular music originating from the Philippines. It encompasses a wide range of musical styles and genres that are uniquely Filipino in expression and cultural influence. OPM includes genres such as pop, rock, ballad, R&B, hip-hop, and folk, among others. It often incorporates Tagalog or Filipino lyrics, although English-language songs are also prevalent. OPM artists and bands have contributed significantly to the Philippine music scene, reflecting local traditions, societal issues, and personal experiences through their music.

    • Alernative, Punk, and Pop Rock

      • Alternative rock, punk rock, and pop rock are distinct subgenres within rock music. Alternative rock, born in the 1980s, offers a diverse range of sounds and styles, often characterized by its experimental and indie approach, exploring themes of individualism and societal critique with bands like Nirvana and Radiohead leading the charge. Punk rock, originating in the 1970s, embodies raw energy and rebellion through fast tempos, aggressive lyrics, and DIY aesthetics, with iconic bands such as The Ramones and The Clash influencing generations with their anti-establishment ethos. Pop rock blends rock's instrumentation with catchy melodies and mainstream appeal, featuring polished production and accessible lyrics that resonate widely, exemplified by artists like The Beatles and Maroon 5. Together, these subgenres showcase rock's versatility and enduring influence across musical landscapes.

  • Books

    • My "Go to" Books:

    • American God's by Neil Gaiman

      • "American Gods" is a novel written by Neil Gaiman, exploring themes of mythology, identity, and belief in contemporary America. The story follows Shadow Moon, a recently released ex-convict who becomes entangled in a conflict between old gods from ancient mythologies and new gods representing modern obsessions such as technology and media. As Shadow navigates this supernatural world, he encounters gods like Odin, Anansi, and Bilquis, each struggling for relevance and power in a changing society where belief shapes reality. Gaiman's narrative weaves together elements of fantasy, folklore, and Americana, offering a thought-provoking exploration of faith, cultural identity, and the evolving nature of worship in a multicultural society.

    • Ayn Rand's The Fountainhead and Atlas Shrugged

      • Ayn Rand was a Russian-American writer and philosopher known for her advocacy of individualism, rational self-interest, and laissez-faire capitalism. Her philosophy, known as Objectivism, is articulated in works such as "Atlas Shrugged" and "The Fountainhead." Rand's novels often feature strong-willed protagonists who embody her philosophical principles, challenging societal norms and advocating for personal achievement and freedom from government interference. Objectivism emphasizes reason as the only means of acquiring knowledge and rejects faith and altruism as moral virtues, asserting that individuals should pursue their own happiness and success. Rand's writings have sparked considerable debate and controversy, influencing discussions on ethics, politics, and the role of the individual in society.

    • The Kingkiller Chronicles

      • "The Kingkiller Chronicle" is a fantasy series written by Patrick Rothfuss, currently comprising two published books: "The Name of the Wind" and "The Wise Man's Fear," with a third book, "The Doors of Stone," anticipated. The series follows the story of Kvothe, a gifted young musician and magician known as the "Kingkiller," as he recounts his life's adventures to a chronicler named Chronicler. Set in a richly detailed world of magic and intrigue, the narrative weaves together Kvothe's journey from a young prodigy seeking knowledge and revenge to his exploits at a renowned magical university and encounters with mythical creatures and powerful beings. Rothfuss's writing is praised for its lyrical prose, complex characters, and intricate world-building, drawing readers into a tale of mystery, romance, and epic fantasy.
